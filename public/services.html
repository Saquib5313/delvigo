Full Truckload freight means your goods have the entire vehicle to themselves - from pickup to delivery. No shared space, no added stops, no waiting for other consignments to be sorted. Just your consignment, moving fast.

is Full Truckload (FTL) Freight?

FTL or Full Truckload shipping is a method where one business books the entire capacity of a truck for a single consignment. Unlike PTL where space is shared, FTL gives you exclusive use of the vehicle.

This makes it the preferred choice for:

  • High-volume consignments where there is enough cargo to fill a truck
  • Time-sensitive deliveries that cannot afford delays from multiple drops
  • Fragile or high-value goods that benefit from direct handling
  • Factory-to-factory or warehouse-to-warehouse bulk movement

Move What You Need. Pay for What You Use.

Part Truckload shipping gives you the cost benefits of shared freight without sacrificing reliability. Your goods travel alongside other cargo, but are handled securely, tracked carefully, and delivered on a structured timeline.

What is Part Truckload (PTL) Freight?

PTL or Part Truckload shipping consolidates multiple smaller consignments from different shippers into one truck. Each business pays only for the space their cargo occupies, making it significantly more economical than booking a full truck.

PTL is the right choice when:

  • Your consignment does not fill an entire truck but needs to move on a regular schedule
  • You need structured, predictable freight at a lower cost per consignment
  • You are an SME shipping mid-sized loads
  • You want reliability without enterprise-level minimums

What is Last-Mile Delivery?

Last-mile delivery refers to the movement of goods from a warehouse, sorting center, or fulfillment point to the final delivery address.

It is called 'last-mile' because it represents the final link in the supply chain. It is also typically the most expensive and complex part of logistics.
